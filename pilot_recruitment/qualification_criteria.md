# LeadFlow Pilot Customer Qualification Criteria

## Target Profile

### Business Type
- **Industry**: Service-based businesses (home services, professional services, consulting, agencies)
- **Size**: 1-10 employees (owner-operators to small teams)
- **Location**: North America (US and Canada)
- **Annual Revenue**: $100K - $2M

### Pain Points (Must Have at Least 2)
- [ ] Leads slipping through the cracks (no follow-up system)
- [ ] Using spreadsheets or notebooks to track leads
- [ ] Missing calls/emails from potential customers
- [ ] No centralized place to see all incoming inquiries
- [ ] Spending time on unqualified leads
- [ ] No-shows for scheduled consultations/demos
- [ ] Manual scheduling back-and-forth wasting time
- [ ] Difficulty tracking which marketing channels work

### Ideal Indicators
- Currently using: Spreadsheets, paper notes, sticky notes, basic CRM, or nothing
- Active on at least one marketing channel (Google, Facebook, Instagram, referrals)
- Owner is tech-comfortable but not tech-savvy (uses smartphone apps regularly)
- Has expressed frustration about "losing leads" in conversations or reviews
- Recently tried to grow but felt disorganized

### Red Flags (Disqualify)
- Already using a robust CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive)
- Large enterprise with dedicated sales team
- No online presence (no website, no Google Business Profile)
- Not the decision maker (can't approve new tools)

### Qualification Questions
1. How do you currently track leads that come in?
2. What happens when you miss a call from a potential customer?
3. How many leads would you estimate you lost last month?
4. What would it mean to your business if you converted 20% more leads?

### Scoring
- **Tier 1 (Ideal)**: 4+ pain points, owner-operator, actively trying to grow
- **Tier 2 (Good)**: 2-3 pain points, small team, open to new tools
- **Tier 3 (Maybe)**: 1-2 pain points, need education on value
