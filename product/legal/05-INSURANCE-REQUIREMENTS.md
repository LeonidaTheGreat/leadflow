## 1. Technology Errors & Omissions (E&O) Insurance

### 1.1 Purpose & Coverage

**What it covers:**
- Errors or omissions in software/service performance
- Claims that the Service failed or malfunctioned
- AI-generated content defects or inaccuracies
- Professional liability for advice/guidance provided
- Copyright/IP infringement in deliverables
- Legal defense costs
- Regulatory fines (up to policy limit)

**What it does NOT cover:**
- Criminal conduct or willful violations
- Contractual liability (unless specifically endorsed)
- Bodily injury or property damage (use General Liability)
- Employment practices (use EPLI)

### 1.2 Recommended Coverage Limits

**For AI Lead Response System:**
- **General Limit:** $1,000,000 per claim / $3,000,000 aggregate
- **Beta/Limited Launch:** $500K-$1M sufficient
- **Scale (1,000+ users):** $3M-$5M recommended

**Deductible:** $25,000 - $50,000 (balances premium vs. out-of-pocket)

### 1.3 Key Endorsements to Include

**AI/Automation Rider:**
- [ ] Specific coverage for AI-generated content errors
- [ ] Coverage for algorithmic bias claims
- [ ] Coverage for AI model defects

**TCPA/Regulatory Rider:**
- [ ] Coverage for TCPA violations (if lead generation is outsourced)
- [ ] Coverage for regulatory fines and penalties
- [ ] Does NOT cover willful violations

**Data/Privacy Rider:**
- [ ] Coverage for privacy policy breaches
- [ ] Coverage for unintended data disclosures
- [ ] Note: Cyber Liability usually primary for data breach costs

**IP Indemnity:**
- [ ] Defense if third party claims your service infringes their IP
- [ ] Coverage if your use of APIs violates others' rights

### 1.4 Top E&O Carriers for Tech/SaaS

**Recommended carriers:**
- **Errors & Omissions Group (EOG)** - Tech-focused, AI-aware
- **Silent Partner** - Tech E&O specialists
- **Beazley** - Major E&O writer, good AI coverage
- **Chubb** - Premier carrier, high limits available
- **AIG** - Global, strong E&O portfolio
- **Zurich** - Growing AI/tech specialty

**Where to apply:**
- Direct through carrier websites
- Tech insurance brokers (find via SIIA - Software & Information Industry Association)
- Regional insurance brokers with E&O expertise

### 1.5 Underwriting Questions You'll Face

**Carriers will ask:**
- "What is your annual revenue from the Service?" → Be honest
- "How many users/leads does the Service process monthly?" → Provide projections
- "Have you had any claims or complaints?" → Disclose fully
- "What security measures are in place?" → Reference SOC 2, encryption, etc.
- "Do you have contractual liability limits?" → Reference ToS Section 3
- "Are you processing payment data?" → Answer about payment processor use
- "Do you generate legal/financial advice?" → Clarify you don't; ToS disclaims

**Underwriting timeline:** 1-3 weeks for SaaS coverage

### 1.6 Cost Estimates

**Annual premium for E&O:**

| Scenario | Users | Limit | Est. Premium | Deductible |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Beta Launch | <100 | $1M | $1,500-2,000 | $25K |
| Growth Phase | 100-500 | $2M | $2,500-3,500 | $25K |
| Scale | 500+ | $3M-5M | $4,000-6,000 | $50K |

**Cost drivers:**
- Annual revenue from Service
- Number of transactions/leads processed
- Revenue per customer
- Prior claims history
- Security measures in place
- AI-specific features

## 2. Cyber Liability Insurance

### 2.1 Purpose & Coverage

**What it covers:**
- Data breaches (unauthorized access to user/lead data)
- Business interruption (lost income from system downtime)
- Forensic investigation costs
- Legal notification and credit monitoring costs
- Regulatory fines and penalties (varies by policy)
- Extortion/ransomware demands
- Third-party liability (users suing you for data breach)
- Media liability (claims of privacy invasion)

**What it does NOT cover:**
- Network outages not involving unauthorized access
- Contractual liability (unless endorsed)
- Bodily injury
- IP infringement (use E&O)

### 2.2 Recommended Coverage Limits

**For AI Lead Response System:**
- **Cyber Liability Limit:** $1,000,000 per claim / $2,000,000 aggregate
- **Beta/Limited:** $500K sufficient
- **Scale:** $2M-$3M recommended

**Deductible:** $25,000 - $50,000

**Sub-limits to confirm:**
- Business Interruption: $50,000-$100,000
- Breach notification costs: $50,000-$100,000
- Credit monitoring: $25,000-$50,000
- Regulatory fines: $100,000-$500,000 (if covered)

### 2.3 Key Endorsements to Include

**Breach Notification Costs:**
- [ ] Covers cost of notifying affected users
- [ ] Covers credit monitoring (3 years standard)
- [ ] Covers regulatory notifications

**Regulatory Fines & Penalties:**
- [ ] Coverage for FTC fines (GDPR/CCPA violations)
- [ ] Coverage for state AG penalties
- [ ] Note: Some policies exclude intentional violations

**Network Security Liability:**
- [ ] Third-party liability if your systems harm others' networks
- [ ] Denial-of-service (DDoS) liability

**Cloud Hosting Liability:**
- [ ] Covers cloud provider failures (if endorsed)
- [ ] Specifies what cloud services are covered

**TCPA/Telemarketing:**
- [ ] Coverage for SMS-related data breaches
- [ ] Coverage for unauthorized SMS transmission

### 2.4 Top Cyber Liability Carriers

**Recommended carriers:**
- **Chubb** - Excellent cyber coverage, AI-aware
- **Beazley** - Specialists in cyber liability
- **Zurich** - Strong cloud/SaaS coverage
- **Travelers** - Broad cyber programs
- **AIG** - Global cyber leader
- **Hiscox** - SMB/startup-focused

**Cyber insurance brokers:**
- Panda Security Advisors
- Brown & Brown (cyber division)
- Mercer (cyber specialists)
- Contact through ISSA (Information Systems Security Association)

### 2.5 Underwriting Questions

**Carriers will ask:**
- "What data do you store and for how long?" → Lead info, 90-day retention, etc.
- "What encryption do you use?" → AES-256, TLS 1.2+
- "Have you had a breach?" → Disclose all incidents
- "Who accesses the systems?" → Number of employees, contractors
- "What's your backup/disaster recovery plan?" → Describe backup procedures
- "Do you conduct security assessments?" → Reference SOC 2, penetration testing
- "Who are your sub-processors?" → List cloud provider, SMS gateway, API providers
- "How do you handle user data deletion?" → Describe retention and deletion procedures

**Common requirements:**
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) for admin access
- Annual security audit or SOC 2 Type II
- Incident response plan (you may need to provide)
- Data minimization practices
- Encryption requirements

### 2.6 Cost Estimates

**Annual premium for Cyber Liability:**

| Scenario | Users | Limit | Est. Premium | Deductible |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Beta | <100 | $500K | $800-1,200 | $25K |
| Growth | 100-500 | $1M | $1,500-2,000 | $25K |
| Scale | 500+ | $2M | $2,500-3,500 | $50K |

**Cost drivers:**
- Number of users and leads stored
- Annual revenue
- Security certifications (SOC 2 = lower premium)
- Prior breach history
- Cloud infrastructure location
- Data retention policies

## 3. General Liability Insurance

### 3.1 Purpose & Coverage

**What it covers:**
- Bodily injury (if someone is injured on your premises)
- Property damage (damage to others' property)
- Personal injury (defamation, invasion of privacy, wrongful eviction)
- Advertising injury (copyright/trademark in marketing)
- Medical payments (small injuries on premises)
- Legal defense costs

**What it does NOT cover:**
- Professional services errors (use E&O)
- Data breaches (use Cyber Liability)
- Employment issues (use EPLI)
- Intentional harm

### 3.2 Recommended Coverage Limits

**For AI Lead Response System:**
- **Bodily Injury/Property Damage:** $1,000,000 per occurrence / $2,000,000 general aggregate
- **Personal & Advertising Injury:** $1,000,000
- **Medical Payments:** $5,000-$10,000 (if office-based)

**Deductible:** $1,000 - $5,000

### 3.3 Key Endorsements

**Contractual Liability:**
- [ ] Covers liability assumed under contracts (may be needed per ToS)

**Products/Completed Operations:**
- [ ] Covers injuries from delivered services (may apply to Service)

**Tenant User Liability:**
- [ ] If office is leased (check lease requirements)

### 3.4 Top General Liability Carriers

**Recommended carriers:**
- **Hiscox** - SMB/startup-focused
- **Nationwide** - Broad coverage
- **Progressive/Travelers** - Competitive rates
- **Hartford** - Strong GL programs

**Where to get quotes:**
- Local independent insurance agent
- Online: Insureon, Bunker
- Direct from carrier websites

### 3.5 Cost Estimates

**Annual premium for General Liability:**

| Limit | Annual Premium | Deductible |
|---|---|---|
| $1M | $400-800 | $1K |
| $2M | $600-1,200 | $5K |

**Cost drivers:**
- Number of employees
- Office location and size
- Nature of business (lower risk for SaaS)
- Prior claims history

## 7. Insurance Policy Checklist

### 7.1 Before You Purchase

- [ ] Define your revenue projections and number of users
- [ ] Collect your security documentation (SOC 2, data flow diagram, etc.)
- [ ] List all sub-processors and integrations (cloud, SMS, CRM, AI)
- [ ] Prepare a business description for underwriting
- [ ] Identify your data retention and deletion policies
- [ ] Document your TCPA and GDPR/CCPA compliance procedures

### 7.2 Comparing Policy Quotes

**Key questions to ask each carrier:**

1. **Are AI-generated content errors covered?**
   - Get written clarification (not just verbal)

2. **Is TCPA/telemarketing coverage included?**
   - Confirm coverage limits and exclusions

3. **Are your sub-processors covered (Claude API, Twilio, etc.)?**
   - Confirm AI services are not excluded

4. **What's the timeline for claim payment?**
   - Standard is 30-60 days; confirm no delays

5. **Is there a separate cyber endorsement?**
   - Some E&O includes cyber; confirm bundling/limitations

6. **What are the exclusions?**
   - Ask specifically about: intentional violations, regulatory fines, contractual liability

7. **Do you have an AI/automation rider?**
   - More carriers are adding these; request it

8. **What's the renewal process and rate lock?**
   - Confirm multi-year options for stability

### 7.3 Documentation to Provide

**Underwriters will request:**

1. **Company Information:**
   - Business plan and revenue projections
   - Organization chart and officer bios
   - Prior insurance claims (if any)
   - Financial statements (if available)

2. **Product/Service Description:**
   - Detailed description of AI Lead Response System
   - Who are your users (real estate agents)
   - How is data processed and stored
   - What AI models do you use (Claude API)
   - Who has access to data (employees, integrations)

3. **Security Documentation:**
   - SOC 2 Type II report (if available)
   - Security audit results
   - Data encryption methods
   - Access control procedures
   - Disaster recovery/backup procedures
   - Incident response plan (draft if needed)

4. **Integration Information:**
   - List all third-party integrations (Follow Up Boss, Twilio, Anthropic Claude)
   - Data flow diagram
   - Sub-processor data processing agreements
   - Explain data shared with each

5. **Compliance Documentation:**
   - GDPR/CCPA Privacy Policy (they'll review)
   - TCPA compliance checklist (from Section 4 above)
   - Data retention policies
   - User consent procedures
   - Evidence of consent (sample forms)

6. **Product Documentation:**
   - ToS and Privacy Policy (required)
   - Screenshots/demo of product
   - Marketing materials (shows how product is positioned)
   - User feedback or case studies (if available)

## 10. What To Do If You Get a Claim

### 10.1 Immediate Steps

1. **Notify Your Insurance Broker IMMEDIATELY**
   - Don't wait; time limits matter
   - Provide notice within 24 hours of incident discovery

2. **Preserve Evidence**
   - Keep all emails, logs, and communications
   - Don't modify or delete data related to claim
   - Photograph/preserve physical evidence

3. **Notify Affected Users**
   - If data breach: Notify users per law (usually within 30-90 days)
   - Document all notifications
   - Keep copies for insurance claim

4. **Investigate**
   - Retain forensic investigator (ask insurance company for list)
   - Document root cause and remediation
   - Keep detailed incident log

5. **Document Everything**
   - Timeline of discovery and response
   - All communications with affected parties
   - Costs incurred (investigation, notification, etc.)
   - Insurance carrier contacts and claim number

### 10.2 Claims Process

- Insurance company assigns adjuster
- Adjuster investigates claim
- You may need to participate in depositions
- Settlement negotiations occur
- Claim paid (typically 30-60 days after settlement)

## 12. Insurance Best Practices

### 12.1 Ongoing Risk Management

To reduce premiums and ensure coverage:

1. **Maintain Security Standards**
   - Achieve SOC 2 Type II within first year (reduces E&O premium 10-15%)
   - Conduct annual penetration testing
   - Maintain multi-factor authentication
   - Encrypt all sensitive data

2. **Implement Compliance Procedures**
   - Document TCPA, GDPR, CCPA compliance
   - Train employees on data protection
   - Maintain audit trails
   - Respond quickly to incidents

3. **Keep Policies Updated**
   - Notify insurance company of major changes
   - Update coverage when adding services/integrations
   - Report claims promptly (even small ones)
   - Renew before expiration

4. **Monitor Sub-Processors**
   - Ensure Anthropic (Claude) maintains security
   - Verify SMS provider compliance
   - Confirm cloud provider SLA and security
   - Monitor for service changes

### 12.2 Risk Mitigation

1. **Contractual Protection**
   - Include strong limitation of liability in ToS
   - Require user consent in Privacy Policy
   - Disclaim AI-generated content accuracy
   - Document user responsibilities

2. **Operational Controls**
   - Implement incident response plan
   - Maintain backup/disaster recovery
   - Use version control and rollback capabilities
   - Conduct regular security drills

3. **Documentation**
   - Keep audit trails of all activities
   - Document all user consents
   - Maintain testing and QA procedures
   - Record all incidents and resolutions

## 13. Red Flags & Exclusions to Watch

### 13.1 Common Insurance Exclusions

**Watch out for policies that exclude:**
- ❌ AI-generated content (verify inclusion)
- ❌ SMS/TCPA violations (verify coverage)
- ❌ Regulatory fines (check what's covered)
- ❌ Contractual liability (get endorsement if needed)
- ❌ Your sub-processors (ensure Claude/Twilio/cloud are covered)
- ❌ Privacy violations (cyber liability usually primary)

### 13.2 Policy Gaps to Avoid

When reviewing quotes, confirm:
- [ ] E&O specifically covers AI services
- [ ] Cyber coverage includes breach notification costs
- [ ] GL includes advertising injury for marketing
- [ ] All integrations (Claude, Twilio, etc.) are covered
- [ ] TCPA coverage is included (in E&O or Cyber)
- [ ] Regulatory fines have reasonable limits
